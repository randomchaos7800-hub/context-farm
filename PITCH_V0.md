# Context Farm Pitch v0

Status: draft  
Date: 2026-05-28

## One-Line Pitch

Context Farm turns tribal business knowledge into grounded operational context for humans and AI agents.

## Short Pitch

In small businesses, the problem is usually not missing information. It is that too much important knowledge lives in one person's head.

Context Farm captures that knowledge from documents, notes, transcripts, and direct operator input, structures it into usable domain context, and makes it available through search, grounded Q&A, and agent APIs.

The result is less interruption, faster onboarding, more consistent execution, and better AI automation.

## The Problem

In teams under 100 people, one operator or lead often knows:
- how the process really works
- which exceptions matter
- which documents are actually authoritative
- what thresholds trigger action
- what people keep getting wrong

That creates:
- repeated questions
- brittle onboarding
- inconsistent process execution
- AI agents that can read the handbook but still make bad decisions

## The Core Insight

Most knowledge tools help you find documents.

That is not enough.

What small teams actually need is a way to turn scattered operational material and one person's working knowledge into grounded, reusable context.

Context Farm is built for that job.

## What Context Farm Does

Context Farm:
- ingests messy business knowledge
- seeds a domain from plain-English operator knowledge
- compiles procedures, constraints, exceptions, and decisions
- preserves provenance and authority
- serves answers to both humans and AI agents

It is not just a wiki, not just a vector store, and not just chat over files.

## Why Now

Two things are happening at once:

1. small teams are leaning harder on a few key operators
2. AI agents are being asked to operate without enough grounded business context

Both problems have the same root cause: operational knowledge is concentrated but not well structured.

## Why This Is Credible

This is not a greenfield idea.

The product is being built on top of:
- months of real internal extraction and knowledge-capture use
- a working compile pipeline
- live ingestion and grounded Q&A infrastructure
- actual operational usage in finance, research, and infrastructure domains

The problem emerged from doing the work, not brainstorming from a blank page.

## Best Initial Users

- founder-led service businesses
- small operations-heavy teams
- finance/admin-heavy SMBs
- agencies
- internal teams where one lead holds too much process knowledge
- AI-heavy teams coordinating multiple agents

## Product Outcome

When Context Farm works, a team gets:
- fewer interruptions to the lead operator
- faster answers for staff
- more consistency in edge cases
- shorter onboarding time
- a shared operational memory layer for AI agents

## Positioning Lines

Primary:

"Context Farm turns tribal business knowledge into grounded operational context for humans and AI agents."

Alternates:

- "Operational memory for small teams."
- "Capture what your lead operator knows before it becomes a bottleneck."
- "Ground your team and AI agents in how your business actually works."

## What It Is Not

- not enterprise search
- not generic RAG
- not a heavyweight ontology platform
- not another document pile with a chatbot on top

## Wedge

The wedge is key-person dependency reduction.

That makes the product legible, painful, and urgent for the right teams.

## Demo Thesis

The best demo is not "look how smart the model is."

The best demo is:

1. ingest messy operational material
2. capture the lead's unwritten rules
3. ask a question that normally requires that person
4. show a grounded answer with the rule, the exception, and the source

That demonstrates the product immediately.
