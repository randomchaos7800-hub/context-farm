## Problem Section

### Heading

Most small teams do not have a documentation problem.

### Body

They have a knowledge concentration problem.

The real process knowledge usually lives in one person's head:
- the exceptions
- the thresholds
- the caveats
- the unofficial but necessary operating rules

That slows onboarding, creates repeated interruptions, and makes AI automation brittle.

## Solution Section

### Heading

Capture how the business actually works.

### Body

Context Farm ingests documents, notes, transcripts, and operator knowledge, then compiles them into grounded operational context:
- procedures
- constraints
- exceptions
- decisions
- source-backed facts

Search it. Ask questions against it. Feed it to your agent fleet.

## How It Works

### 1. Ingest

Bring in PDFs, URLs, notes, transcripts, and raw business knowledge.

### 2. Seed

Describe the domain in plain English so the system knows what matters.

### 3. Compile

Turn messy inputs into structured, traceable operational knowledge.

### 4. Serve

Return grounded answers and briefings for staff and AI agents.

## Why It Matters

### Heading

The team stops depending on memory as infrastructure.

### Body

Context Farm helps:
- reduce repeated operator interruptions
- speed up onboarding
- improve consistency in edge cases
- give AI agents the actual rules, not just documents

## For Humans And Agents

### Heading

One operational memory layer. Two consumers.

### Body

Humans use Context Farm to search, ask, and get briefed.

Agents use Context Farm through APIs to retrieve constraints, procedures, and approved context before acting.
